# About Me

I am a doctoral researcher at [Umeå University](https://www.umu.se/en/) in the [Software Engineering and Security Group](https://www.umu.se/en/research/groups/software-security-/), supervised by [Prof. Alexandre Bartel](https://abartel.net). My research focus is on Java deserialization vulnerabilities, including static vulnerability analysis, fuzzing, reverse engineering, and mitigation techniques. Being funded by the Swedish [WASP](https://wasp-sweden.org/) research initiative, I also had the opportunity to collaborate with [Prof. Patrick McDaniel](https://patrickmcdaniel.org/) in the [UW Madison S&P group](https://madsp.cs.wisc.edu/) and [Prof. Caroline LeMieux](https://www.carolemieux.com/) at [UBC](https://spl.cs.ubc.ca/).

Before starting my doctoral project, I worked as a Security Analyst in the Security Operations Center of the [NOW IT GmbH](https://www.now-it.de/), a major IT service provider of the German federal pension insurance.

Outside of work, I am an amateur photgrapher, fitness enthusiast, and boardgame & MTG geek.
